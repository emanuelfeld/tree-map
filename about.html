About

Wish there were more trees in your DC neighborhood?

Residents can suggest new planting locations to DDOT's Urban Forestry Administration until June 15th. UFA will then prioritize requested areas in its Fall planting season.

This site can help you investigate where there are empty tree boxes near you.

Just search for your address, click on one of the empty plots (shown as orange points on the map), and use the link provided in the pop-up to submit a request through 311.dc.gov.

Before asking for a new tree, be sure to double check that the space hasn't already been filled by walking over to the location or by checking it out using the Google Street View link.

More information

The map on the homepage was built using the UFA Street Trees and 2012 ward boundaries datasets from opendata.dc.gov and the neighborhood boundaries file from Code for DC's data catalog.

In the site's logo you'll find the outline of a leaf from a Sugar Maple tree, one of the most common in the District.
